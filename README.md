## Project 01

The story that is being told across the four plots is about what being a dominant player in soccer means and how dominance can vary across positions. 
Instead of making a blanket statement of one player being the best, the goal was to utilize visualizations to determine which players were the best in their specific role. 
When looking across the four positions what makes a player dominant is not completely straightforward. 
For example, in regard to Goalkeepers, Enyeama has the same number of saves as Kingson and Neuer with 20 saves however Enyema did this while only playing 270 minutes compared to Kingson’s 510 minutes and Neuer’s 540. 
Having the same number of saves while playing less time would contribute to a Goalkeeper being more dominant in their position. 
Another example of this would be Schweinsteiger and Alonso as Midfielders. Neither of these players are particularly dominant in passes or tackles however were very strong in both.  
Having a player be competent in both passes and tackles would makes this player a more dominant player than a player who is strong in only one of these skills. 
The goal of the four visualization was to show standout players by looking at the context of their position and the skills required for their position.

## Project 02

Across the three visualizations my goal was to show the story of how two teams were incredibly dominant from 1980 through to 2018. 
When reviewing the NBA Championship Cities map it is clear the two points are larger than the others, these two teams are the Los Angeles Lakers and the Chicago Bulls. 
The Lakers have won a total of 10 NBA Championships and the Bulls have won a total of 6 NBA Championships. 
When looking at the Average Points Scored by NBA Champions Per Year scatter plot we can see that there is a cluster of Bulls championships in the 1990s. 
This was due to the fact that the Bulls had a dominant team built on Michael Jordan and Scottie Pippen during this era where they were able to win 6 championships. 
When looking at the Average Points Scored by NBA Champions Per Year scatter plot for the Lakers we can see there are two clusters of championships wins one during the 1980s and one during the early 2000s.
This was due to the fact that the Lakers had a dominant player in Magic Johnson during the 1980s and an all star duo of Shaq and Kobe Bryant during the 2000s. 
The Lakers were able to put together two incredibly dominant rosters that lead them to 10 NBA Championships, which is what differentiates them every other team.

## Project 03

In this project, I explored the data for Tampa International Airport (TPA) from FSU's Florida Climate Center.
When analyzing this data the primary goal was to look at temperature and precipitation data from 2022.
I recreated graphs that looked at how temperatures fluctuated over the course of the year, and also looked at daily rainfall amount throughout 2022.
In addition to analyzing TPA weather patterns, I also looked at the Billboard Top 100 song lyrics data.
I utilized this data set to create a word cloud visualizing most common words in these songs while removing very common words such as "and" and "the".
